# Outfit Matching Guide

This guide explains how Wardrobe Wizard should match outfits to common situations.

Wardrobe Wizard should recommend practical outfits from the available wardrobe items only. It should not suggest buying new clothes in the MVP.

## General Matching Principles

A good outfit recommendation should consider:

* the user's situation,
* weather or context,
* comfort level,
* selected style vibes,
* available wardrobe items,
* formality,
* practical constraints.

The recommendation should include:

* a main outfit,
* an alternative outfit,
* a short explanation of why the outfit works.

The explanation should be warm, practical, and concise.

## Office Rainy Day

For an office rainy day, the assistant should prioritize:

* weather protection,
* closed shoes,
* layers such as trench coats, blazers, coats, or cardigans,
* office-friendly or elegant style tags,
* medium or high comfort,
* polished but realistic combinations.

Avoid:

* open shoes,
* very low-comfort items,
* outfits that feel too casual for work,
* explanations focused only on appearance.

A strong office rainy day outfit may include:

* a blouse or simple top,
* tailored trousers or a practical dress,
* a trench coat or blazer,
* ankle boots or loafers,
* a practical bag.

## Date Night

For date night, the assistant should prioritize:

* elegant, romantic, feminine, masculine, or personal style vibes selected by the user,
* smart-casual or elegant formality,
* comfort that still allows the user to feel relaxed,
* one stronger visual element, such as satin, a dress, a polished shirt, or elegant shoes.

Avoid:

* suggesting gender-based styling rules,
* assuming the user's age or gender,
* recommending new purchases,
* making the outfit too formal if the user prefers comfort.

A strong date night outfit may include:

* a satin top with trousers,
* a midi dress with a jacket,
* elegant shoes,
* a small bag or accessory.

## Travel Day

For a travel day, the assistant should prioritize:

* high comfort,
* travel-friendly style tags,
* layers,
* shoes suitable for walking,
* simple items that are easy to combine,
* practical accessories.

Avoid:

* low-comfort shoes,
* fragile or difficult-to-wear items,
* overly formal combinations,
* outfits that do not support movement.

A strong travel day outfit may include:

* a comfortable top,
* jeans or relaxed trousers,
* sneakers,
* a cardigan, jacket, or coat,
* a practical bag or scarf.

## Custom Scenario

For a custom scenario, the assistant should read the user's text and look for clues such as:

* rain,
* cold,
* warm weather,
* office,
* date,
* dinner,
* travel,
* walking,
* comfort,
* elegant,
* casual.

If the custom scenario is vague, the assistant should choose a balanced practical outfit and explain the assumption clearly.

## Explanation Style

The explanation should answer:

* why the outfit fits the situation,
* why it matches the selected style vibes,
* why it is practical for comfort or weather,
* how the alternative outfit differs from the main outfit.

The explanation should not:

* mention gender identity,
* suggest buying new clothes,
* shame the user's preferences,
* overpromise fashion expertise,
* claim that the outfit is objectively perfect.
